Chicago neighborhood guide

west town.

West Town is a broad, character-filled area that includes several beloved micro-neighborhoods — offering a mix of classic Chicago housing, local businesses, and easy access to downtown.

Area vibe
Eclectic · local · residential-urban mix
Housing mix
Two- and three-flats · walk-ups · condos · single-family homes
Ideal for
Renters & buyers who want neighborhood character with quick access to other hot spots
Transit access
Multiple bus routes · nearby CTA Blue Line · easy access to 90/94

Life in West Town

West Town covers several overlapping pockets — each with its own energy — but they share a common thread: local businesses, classic Chicago streets, and a strong neighborhood feel.

You’ll find restaurants, cafés, bars, boutiques, and long-standing mom-and-pop shops woven between residential blocks. Many streets have that classic Chicago feel: tree-lined, with brick and frame buildings, front porches, and walk-up entrances.

West Town is well-positioned between downtown and many popular west side and northwest side neighborhoods, making it an appealing option for people who want flexibility — close to the action, but not right on top of it.

Who this area works well for

  • Renters who want value, space, and neighborhood character
  • Buyers seeking condos, multi-units, or single-family homes
  • Residents who like being near hot spots like Wicker Park, Ukrainian Village, and West Loop without being in the busiest corridors

Homes & rentals in West Town

West Town offers a wide range of options — from vintage rentals to newer condos and updated single-family homes — often with more space for the price than trendier nearby pockets.

What you’ll typically see

  • Vintage two- and three-flats with classic layouts
  • Condo conversions and newer boutique buildings
  • Single-family homes and multi-units on quieter residential blocks
