Chicago neighborhood guide

west town.

A broad, character-rich area made up of distinct pockets (including Ukrainian Village and parts of East Village) — known for classic Chicago housing, local dining, and an easy connection to Wicker Park, the West Loop, and downtown.

Life in West Town

West Town is loved for its “best of both worlds” mix: residential streets with classic Chicago homes, plus lively corridors with great restaurants, bars, and everyday essentials. Because West Town is made up of multiple pockets, the vibe can shift from block to block — from quiet, tree-lined side streets to busier dining strips — but it consistently feels local, walkable, and close to everything.

Shops & dining

Dining and local favorites are spread across a few main corridors — perfect for “walk to dinner” living with plenty of neighborhood staples in between.

Homes & feel

A classic Chicago mix: brick two- and three-flats, vintage walk-ups, rehabbed properties, and some newer condo builds — often on quiet side streets.

Commute & access

Convenient to downtown via multiple bus routes and nearby CTA train access depending on the pocket — plus easy bike routes into Wicker Park, River West, and the West Loop.
