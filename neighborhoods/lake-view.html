Life in Lake View

Lake View is one of Chicago’s most active and varied neighborhoods, offering something different block by block.

Corridors like Broadway, Clark, and Southport offer endless restaurants, cafés, bars, shops, and everyday conveniences. The lakefront path, Belmont Harbor, and parks frame the eastern side of the neighborhood, giving residents plenty of outdoor activity space.

Despite the density, many streets in Lake View feel quiet and residential, lined with walk-ups, courtyard buildings, and condos. It’s a flexible neighborhood that appeals to renters, first-time buyers, and long-term residents alike.

Who this area works well for

  • Renters who want nightlife, restaurants, and transit access
  • Buyers who want more space than central neighborhoods offer
  • Residents who value outdoor recreation and lake proximity

Homes & rentals in Lake View

Lake View's housing ranges from affordable rentals to renovated condos and lakefront high-rises — making it a versatile option at many price points.

What you’ll typically see

  • Classic courtyard and walk-up rentals
  • Condo conversions and newer mid-rise buildings
  • High-rise rentals and condos with lake or skyline views
