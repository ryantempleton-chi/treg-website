avondale.

A Northwest Side pocket with classic two- and three-flats, tree-lined streets, and a growing lineup of local restaurants, bars, and cafés — close to Logan Square, but with a more laid-back pace.

Life in Avondale

Avondale feels like classic Chicago — calm residential blocks, corner staples, and a steady stream of new local spots that make it easy to stay close to home. It’s a neighborhood many buyers and renters choose for value, space, and proximity to the Blue Line and nearby favorites.

Food & local spots

Breweries, neighborhood restaurants, and newer openings along Milwaukee and Belmont — with plenty of low-key favorites in between.

Homes & feel

Classic two- and three-flats, walk-up condos, and single-family homes — often more space for the money than nearby hotspots.

Commute & access

Easy Kennedy access, bus routes throughout the area, and quick connections into Logan Square and downtown via nearby Blue Line stops.

Neighborhood snapshot

Where you’ll find Avondale
Northwest Side · near the Kennedy · along Milwaukee, Belmont, and Kedzie
Local orientation
  • Nearby: Logan Square · Irving Park · Roscoe Village
  • Boundaries: Northwest Side pocket along the Kennedy and Milwaukee Ave corridor
  • Good to know: Avondale is often cross-shopped with Logan Square for similar access and amenities — usually with a quieter, more residential feel.
  • Tip: If Blue Line proximity matters most, compare options by distance to Logan Square, Belmont, and Irving Park stations.
