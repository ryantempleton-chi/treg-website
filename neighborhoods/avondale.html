Life in Avondale

Avondale offers a comfortable, residential feel while still being connected to the rest of the city — with a growing lineup of local spots and easy access to nearby hot spots.

Many blocks in Avondale feel like classic Chicago — tree-lined streets, brick two-flats, and longtime residents mixed with newer arrivals. Over the years, more restaurants, coffee shops, and breweries have opened in and around the neighborhood, adding energy without overwhelming the residential core.

Its location along the Blue Line and close to the expressway makes it appealing for commuters who want to balance city access with a quieter home base and more space for their budget.

Who this area works well for

  • Renters looking for more space and value than core North Side neighborhoods
  • Buyers considering condos, two-flats, or single-family homes
  • Residents who want a relaxed neighborhood feel with growing restaurant and café options nearby

Homes & rentals in Avondale

Avondale offers a range of housing options — including vintage rentals, condo conversions, and single-family homes — often at more approachable prices than some neighboring areas.

What you’ll typically see

  • Vintage two- and three-flat rentals with classic layouts
  • Condo conversions in older buildings and smaller walk-ups
  • Single-family homes and bungalows on quieter residential blocks
