Chicago neighborhood guide

river north

River North is a true downtown-adjacent neighborhood — high-rises, gallery spaces, and nightlife all packed into a walkable grid just north of the river. It’s city living with quick access to the Loop, the lakefront, and everything in between.

Area vibe
Energetic · urban · nightlife & dining
Housing mix
High-rise rentals, condos, select townhomes
Ideal for
Professionals, city-first lifestyles, people who want to walk to work
Transit access
Multiple CTA lines, quick access to the Loop & express buses

Life in River North

Life in River North moves quickly. By day, you’ll see professionals heading to offices downtown or working from cafés; by night, the neighborhood turns into a destination for dining, nightlife, and rooftop views of the skyline.

River North is one of Chicago’s most urban-feeling neighborhoods, with glass towers, hotel bars, galleries, and restaurants lining the main streets. The Riverwalk, Michigan Avenue, and the Loop are all just a short walk away, making it especially appealing for those who want to minimize their commute and maximize their time in the city.

Despite the energy, there are pockets of calm on side streets and near the river, where you can step back from the noise without leaving the neighborhood. It’s a strong fit if you want amenities, convenience, and a city skyline out your window.

Who this area works well for

  • Professionals who want to walk to work or ride a short commute
  • Renters and buyers who value amenities and views
  • Anyone who wants to be close to the river, downtown, and nightlife without being in the Loop itself

Homes & rentals in River North

River North offers a wide range of rental and ownership options — most of them vertical — with building amenities and views often playing a big role in the decision.

What you’ll typically see

  • High-rise rental buildings with gyms, lounges, rooftops, and other full-amenity packages
  • Condominium towers with a mix of full-time residents and part-time or pied-à-terre owners
  • A limited number of townhomes and low-rise options tucked on quieter blocks
