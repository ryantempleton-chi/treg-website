Life in the Loop

The Loop is the center of the city — with a weekday energy driven by offices and commuters, and a growing residential base that keeps evenings and weekends active as well.

Residents are close to Millennium Park, the Theater District, the Riverwalk, museums, restaurants, and major employers. It’s one of the most convenient locations in the city for transit, walkability, and access to other neighborhoods.

While the Loop has traditionally been an office district, more residential buildings and adaptive reuse projects have added apartments, condos, and loft-style spaces to the mix.

Who this area works well for

  • Professionals who want to walk to work and transit
  • Renters seeking newer apartments in the heart of downtown
  • Buyers looking at condos or loft-style units in a central location

Homes & rentals in the Loop

Housing in the Loop is largely high-rise based — with a mix of newer apartments, condo buildings, and converted historic properties offering residential options downtown.

What you’ll typically see

  • Newer apartment towers with full amenities
  • Condo buildings with city and river views
  • Loft-style units and historic conversions in select buildings
