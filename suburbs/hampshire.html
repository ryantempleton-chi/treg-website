A far Northwest option for buyers prioritizing space, quiet streets, and value — with a more rural-suburban feel and quick access to nearby hubs for everyday errands.

Life in Hampshire

Hampshire is for people who want room to spread out. You’ll find more open space, a slower pace, and housing that often stretches your budget further than closer-in suburbs. It’s a good fit if you’re comfortable trading a longer commute for quieter living and a bit more breathing room.

Everyday basics

More “run errands in nearby corridors” than a dense downtown scene — with local staples and easy access to larger shopping areas in adjacent suburbs.

Homes & space

Newer subdivisions mixed with larger lots and a more open feel — often a value-driven pick for buyers who want square footage and yard space.

Access & commute

Driving-focused for most commutes. If you commute to Chicago regularly, plan on a longer trip — but if you’re suburban-to-suburban, it can work well depending on your routes.
