Chicagoland suburb guide

chicago suburbs.

Looking outside the city? These suburb guides are a starting point — a quick way to compare communities by feel, commute, housing stock, schools, and day-to-day lifestyle.

Not sure where to begin? Tell us what matters most (commute, walkability, schools, yard space, budget) and we’ll help you narrow the list.

city neighborhoods suburbs
naperville.
A top choice for space + schools — with a walkable downtown, strong dining scene, and Metra access.
schaumburg.
Northwest hub with major shopping, corporate campuses, and a wide range of housing options.
oak park.
Historic architecture, walkable blocks, CTA + Metra access — a city-like feel just west of Chicago.
arlington heights.
Popular Northwest suburb with a strong downtown, Metra access, and a classic suburban mix of homes.
evanston.
Lakefront suburb with urban energy, great dining, and CTA + Metra — anchored by Northwestern.
highland park.
North Shore living with beach access, ravines, and a mix of classic and newer homes.
lake forest.
Estate-style homes, wooded lots, and a quiet North Shore feel — with Metra access downtown.
kenilworth.
Small, prestigious North Shore community with classic homes, lakefront access, and a calm vibe.
winnetka.
Iconic North Shore suburb with beautiful lakefront parks, top schools, and high-end housing.
oak brook.
Upscale West suburb known for large lots, executive homes, and major shopping/dining.
wheaton.
DuPage favorite with a strong downtown, Metra access, and a classic “community” feel.
glen ellyn.
Charming downtown, lake + parks, and a strong housing mix — popular for commuters and families.
lombard.
Central DuPage location with access to highways and Metra nearby — value-oriented housing options.
downers grove.
Commuter-friendly suburb with two Metra stations, a walkable downtown, and a broad housing mix.
orland park.
Southwest suburb with newer housing, shopping corridors, and lots of space for the price.
streamwood.
Northwest value option with parks, trails, and easy access to regional shopping and highways.
bridgeview.
Southwest suburb with quick expressway access and a mix of starter homes and long-time residents.
cicero.
Close-in west suburb with classic Chicago-style housing stock and strong proximity to the city.
mount prospect.
Metra access, a walkable downtown core, and a wide variety of homes at different price points.
river forest.
Leafy, historic homes next to Oak Park — a refined, quiet feel with great city access.
orland hills.
Smaller Southwest community near Orland Park — residential, convenient, and generally quieter.
elmhurst.
Strong schools, a lively downtown, and excellent commuter access — one of the top West suburbs.
brookfield.
Classic Bungalow Belt vibes and great access — plus the Brookfield Zoo nearby.
wheeling.
Diverse housing options and quick access to major roads — a practical North/Northwest pick.
libertyville.
A North Shore-adjacent favorite with a great downtown, parks, and a strong “community” vibe.
st. charles.
Fox River charm with a lively downtown, historic homes, and a strong suburban lifestyle.
palos heights.
Southwest suburb known for forest preserves nearby, a quieter pace, and a residential feel.
lake bluff.
A charming North Shore village with a small downtown, lake access, and a classic community vibe.
oak lawn.
Southwest convenience with a wide range of housing and easy access to city and regional routes.
la grange.
Walkable, charming downtown and Metra access — a top pick for those wanting a “town” feel.
buffalo grove.
Family-friendly North/Northwest suburb with parks, schools, and a strong residential housing mix.
lemont.
Historic downtown + nature access — a great option near Palos preserves with a distinct feel.
hampshire.
More space, more quiet — a far Northwest option for those prioritizing value and room to spread out.
berwyn.
Classic bungalow neighborhoods, close-in convenience, and a strong community feel just west of Chicago.
